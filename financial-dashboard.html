PropertyType Est. Value Q Revenue Q Expenses Q Net Annual Net
Annual Net Cash Flow
YTD Actuals + Avg Quarterly CF Ex-CapEx × Remaining Quarters
Projects your full-year cash flow using real data where it exists. CapEx is excluded from the forward projection since it's non-recurring — prevents one-time purchases from distorting your annual outlook.
Properties Return Metrics
Property Cap Rate (MV)
Capitalization Rate — Market Value
Annualized NOI ÷ Current Market Value
Shows what yield your property generates relative to what it's worth today. Use this to compare against other investments or benchmark against local market cap rates.
 Cap Rate (Cost)
Capitalization Rate — Purchase Price
Annualized NOI ÷ Original Purchase Price
Your locked-in yield at acquisition. Useful for seeing how your original underwriting holds up over time — this number never changes regardless of market swings.
 Cash-on-Cash
Cash-on-Cash Return
Annualized Net CF After P&I ÷ Total Cash Invested
The truest measure of your actual cash return on the money you put in. Accounts for financing — a leveraged property can have a high CoC even with a modest cap rate.
 ROE
Return on Equity
Annualized Net CF ÷ Current Equity (Value − Mortgage)
Measures how hard your equity is working. As properties appreciate and mortgages pay down, ROE tends to fall — signaling when it might make sense to 1031 into a larger asset.
 DSCR
Debt Service Coverage Ratio
Annualized NOI ÷ Annualized P&I Payments
Shows how comfortably income covers debt. ≥1.25x is healthy — lenders typically require this. Below 1.0x means the property can't service its own debt from income alone.
Properties Operating Metrics
Property OER
Operating Expense Ratio
Operating Expenses ÷ Gross Revenue
What share of income goes to expenses before debt service. ≤50% is strong, ≤70% is acceptable, >70% signals the property is consuming too much of its own income.
 Occupancy
Occupancy Rate
STR/MTR: Nights Booked ÷ Nights Available · LTR: Months Occupied ÷ 3
How often your rental is actually generating revenue. Higher is better, but very high occupancy can mean you're underpriced — balance with ADR.
 ADR
Average Daily Rate
STR/MTR: Gross Revenue ÷ Nights Booked · LTR: Gross Revenue ÷ Months Occupied
Your average rate per booked period. For STR/MTR this is nightly; for LTR this is monthly rent per occupied month. Track alongside occupancy — a rising ADR with stable occupancy means your pricing strategy is working.
 RevPAR
Revenue Per Available Night
STR/MTR: Gross Revenue ÷ Nights Available · LTR: Gross Revenue ÷ 3 Months
The single best top-line performance metric for STR/MTR — combines occupancy and rate into one number. Lets you benchmark quarters against each other regardless of how many nights were available.
 Breakeven Occ
Breakeven Occupancy
Total Expenses Incl. P&I ÷ ADR ÷ Nights Available
The occupancy rate you need just to cover all costs. If your actual occupancy is above this, you're profitable. Useful for stress-testing slow seasons and setting pricing floors.
